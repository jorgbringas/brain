
## **Key Activity: The 30-Day Plan**

*   **Nature:** An intensive, guided, and collaborative practice phase. **This is not a formal module.** It begins immediately after the user confirms they understand the theory from Modules 0 & 1.
*   **Pedagogical Goal:** To bridge the massive gap between knowing the theory and applying the enabler and SIR skills correctly and consistently in the user's real life.
*   **Agent's Role:**
    1.  **Diagnose:** Assess how much focused practice the user needs on Rapid Start enablers.
    2.  **Collaborate:** Co-create a personalized 30-Day Plan with the user, using their notes as the foundation.
    3.  **Deepen Knowledge:** Provide context-sensitive, deeper insights into the SIR skills as the user practices them.

### **Structure of the 30-Day Plan Creation Process:**

#### **Step 1: Goals Analysis**
*   **Skill:** Self-Management
*   **Task:** The Agent will prompt the user to define and write down:
    *   **Medium-term Goal (6-12 months):** e.g., "Terminar el curso de ICS y alcanzar un nivel avanzado," "Entrar a la UNI AACHEN."
    *   **30-Day Performance Goals:** e.g., "Follow schedule for at least 10 days in a row," "Practice new techniques for at least 10 hours weekly."
    *   **14-Day Performance Goals:** e.g., "Follow schedule for at least 5 days," "Write manifest."

#### **Step 2: Create a Character - "The Person Who Can Achieve My Goals"**
*   **Skill:** Mindset
*   **Task:** The Agent will guide the user to write down a detailed description of their ideal persona, covering:
    *   **Character:** "una persona con mirada fija, disciplina y sin distracciones."
    *   **Skills:** "Disciplina, foco (0 Distracciones), no procrastinar, Ingenio..."
    *   **Strengths:** "Actuar asertivamente frente a situaciones de mucha presión..."
    *   **Free of Weaknesses:** "Mandar a la mierda todo cuando no sale bien, dejar las cosas a medias..."
    *   **Habits:** "Levantarse temprano, terminar el trabajo a tiempo..."
    *   **Bad Habits Free:** "Flojera, Glotonería, Mucho Netflix..."
    *   **Fear Free:** "Miedo al fracaso, miedo al qué dirán."
    *   **Look:** "Bien aseado, deportivo, ropa minimalista..."
    *   **Judgements Free:** "Discriminar por intelecto..."
    *   **Past Stories Free:** "Miedo al fracaso, cuando vendía libros, días duros..."
    *   **Traits:** "Powerful, smart, driven, classy, discipline."
    *   **Loves to do:** "Tareas de universidad; escribir correos, ensayos; estudiar horas sin parar..."

#### **Step 3: Anticipate Barriers**
*   **Skill:** Self-Management
*   **Task: Time Availability**
    1.  Schedule the time for your goals.
    2.  Identify what puts that time at risk (e.g., "Hambre, no suficiente sueño, choque de horarios con Mimu, Notificaciones").
    3.  Define specific, actionable solutions ("Idear un plan de comidas," "Despertarme más temprano," "Poner el celular en modo no interrumpir").
*   **Task: Limiting Habits**
    1.  Identify the specific habits that sabotage you ("Flojera, Glotonería, Mucho Netflix... Procastrinacion. Sindrome del coleccionista online").
    2.  Identify the trigger-response algorithm ("When [Time pressure] happens, I tend to [estresarme]," "When [Tired] happens, I tend to [take a nap, procrastinate]").

#### **Step 4: Develop Proactive Strategies**
*   **Skill:** Self-Management / Self-Regulation
*   **Task: Scripted Actions**
    *   Create a simple, easy-to-follow protocol for reacting to each limiting habit trigger.
    *   **Example ONLINE:** "Youtube - Web: cerrar ojos y respirar profundamente, bajar volumen."
    *   **Example Hambre:** "levantarme y comer una manzana."
*   **Task: Environmental Optimisations**
    *   Design your physical and digital spaces to promote good habits and hinder bad ones.
    *   **Example Physical:** "Put things away - Put a sign 'I am Studying'."
    *   **Example Digital:** "Usar browsers distintos para estudio y diversion," "Closing tabs," "Organizar apps y esconder las que distraen."

#### **Step 5: Adjustments and Momentum**
*   **Skill:** Self-Management
*   **Task:** The user must define their immediate next steps.
    *   Set practice time for ICS: "30 min daily."
    *   Schedule the next 2 weeks in their calendar.
    *   Commit to a specific practice goal: "Practice Fundamentals 1 - for 3 hours."
